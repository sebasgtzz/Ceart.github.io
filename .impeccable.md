## Design Context

### Users
Prospective students, current students, parents/guardians, and the general public in Mexico (es-MX). They visit the site to learn about music programs, get inspired, and access the student portal. Context: browsed on phones during the day, on desktops at home, often by parents researching options for their children.

### Brand Personality
**Divertido · Educativo · Aspiracional** — fun and alive, rooted in real learning, with a sense that studying here opens doors. Named after Johann Sebastian Bach, which carries inherent prestige and classical depth, but the school isn't stuffy — it's warm and creative.

### Emotional Goal
Visitors should feel **prestige, inspiration, and creativity**. The site should feel like walking into a well-run music school that takes art seriously but isn't intimidating. It should spark the feeling: *"I want to study here."*

### Anti-References
- **No disorder**: Cluttered, chaotic layouts are out
- **No rigid boxes**: Overly corporate, grid-locked, boring card-grid UIs
- **No basic/soulless**: Anything that looks like a generic school website template

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode (the site is primarily browsed in bright contexts). Gold (#ffbb08) as primary accent — warm, musical, aspirational. Ink (#080808) as primary text. Cream (#f9f8f5) as alternate background. Program accent colors (orange, indigo, cyan, red, purple) add playfulness and visual variety. Typography: Playfair Display for display/headings (prestige, artistry), Inter for body (clarity, legibility).

### Design Principles
1. **Aspirational warmth** — Every section should feel elevated but welcoming, never cold or corporate
2. **Musical personality** — Motion, rhythm, and visual energy should echo the feel of music itself
3. **Clarity with flair** — Information is easy to read and navigate, but nothing is plain or forgettable
4. **Prestige through detail** — The polish comes from micro-details: spacing, transitions, hover states, not from being flashy
5. **Spanish-first** — Copy, tone, and hierarchy should feel native to es-MX, not translated

### Scope Notes
- Flagship production site — deep polish is appropriate
- Member portal (student/teacher/admin dashboard) does not yet exist and is a known gap
- Current codebase: vanilla HTML/CSS/JS, inline styles, Firebase Auth, no build system
